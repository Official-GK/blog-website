The House That Watches You Sleep

They said the house was abandoned, but I saw a candle burning in the attic window. The villagers of Ravenmoor warned me not to go near it — “It remembers,” they whispered. I didn’t listen.

The night I stepped inside, the air grew thick with rot and regret. The walls pulsed like they were alive, and shadows slithered across the floor — though I cast no light. A voice, brittle and cold, echoed from upstairs: “You’ve finally returned…”

Every room held relics of a time that shouldn’t exist. A nursery with a cradle that rocked itself. A mirror that showed a reflection with no face. A diary dated 1892, yet freshly written in blood-red ink: “Don’t trust the man in the cellar.”

I tried to leave. But the door I entered through had vanished — replaced by bricks and a whisper: *Stay with us.*

Recommendations

If you ever visit Ravenmoor, avoid the path lit by lanterns. And never — never knock twice.
